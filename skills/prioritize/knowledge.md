# Prioritization & Decision-Making - Knowledge Base

> Curated from 33+ episodes of Lenny's Podcast

## Frameworks

### 1. LNO Framework (Leverage, Neutral, Overhead) - Shreyas Doshi

Categorize every task by return on effort:
- **L tasks:** 10X-100X return. Strategy, key PRDs, critical alignments.
- **N tasks:** 1X return. Standard meetings, routine updates.
- **O tasks:** Less return than effort invested. Expense reports, formatting.

Do L tasks when you're most energetic. Minimize time on O tasks. The same activity can be L, N, or O depending on context.

*Source: episodes/shreyas-doshi*

### 2. Pre-Mortem Framework - Shreyas Doshi

At project inception, imagine it has failed. Work backward using shared vocabulary:
- **Tigers** -- critical threats
- **Paper Tigers** -- perceived but unlikely threats
- **Elephants** -- overlooked concerns nobody discusses

Creates psychological safety for discussing risks upfront.

*Source: episodes/shreyas-doshi*

### 3. Opportunity Cost Over ROI - Shreyas Doshi

In a high-leverage role, stop doing work that simply provides positive ROI. Start focusing on work that minimizes opportunity cost. "You are no longer thinking, 'Is this a good use of my time?' Instead, you are thinking, 'Is this the best use of my time?'"

*Source: episodes/shreyas-doshi*

### 4. Golden Rituals Framework - Shishir Mehrotra (citing Bing Gordon)

Great companies have a small list of named rituals every employee knows by their first Friday. They're templated. Examples: Amazon's 6-pagers, Google's OKRs, Coda's Dory/Pulse meetings. Rituals mirror and embed culture more effectively than stated values.

*Source: episodes/shishir-mehrotra*

### 5. Three Levels of Product Work - Shreyas Doshi

Decisions happen at Impact, Execution, and Optics levels. Most PM-CEO conflicts stem from defaulting to different levels without recognizing the mismatch. PMs default to execution; CEOs think at impact level.

*Source: episodes/shreyas-doshi*

## Common Mistakes

1. **Confusing execution problems with strategy/culture problems.** Teams solve the same "execution problem" with bandaid meetings and processes, when the real issue is unclear strategy or unhealthy culture. (Shreyas Doshi)

2. **Prioritizing positive ROI over opportunity cost.** Teams fill their plate with "quick wins" that look efficient but prevent transformational work. Small denominator creates the illusion of high ROI. (Shreyas Doshi)

3. **Treating all tasks with equal rigor.** Perfectionists waste time polishing expense reports when that effort should be reserved for strategic PRDs. (Shreyas Doshi)

4. **Not creating psychological safety to discuss risks.** "Nobody likes to be negative in modern organizations." Without explicit frameworks like pre-mortems, teams fail to surface threats until post-launch. (Shreyas Doshi)

## Company Examples

**Google** (Shreyas Doshi): "$100M minimum threshold" for ad business opportunities forced the organization to pass on small opportunities and pursue transformational initiatives like video ads.

**Stripe** (Shreyas Doshi): Culture of high energy, sound judgment, low ego, small teams. At Stripe Connect, thinking bigger led to different prioritization and pace.

**Netflix** (Gibson Biddle): Right-sizing content investment based on predicted viewership. $500M for Stranger Things based on 100M predicted viewers. Data-informed decisions, not data-driven.

**Coda** (Shishir Mehrotra): "Dory/Pulse" ritual -- write thoughts before speaking, then vote on questions. Reduces groupthink while maintaining psychological safety.
