# PM Career Growth - Knowledge Base

> Curated from 85+ episodes of Lenny's Podcast

## Frameworks

### 1. PM Competencies Framework - Ravi Mehta (Tinder, Facebook, Tripadvisor)

12 competencies across 4 areas:
- **Product Execution:** Functional specification, delivery, quality
- **Customer Insight:** Data fluency, voice of customer, UX design
- **Product Strategy:** Business outcomes, vision/roadmapping, strategic impact
- **Leadership:** Stakeholder inclusion, team leadership, managing up

Works equally for APMs and CPOs -- the depth changes, not the competencies.

*Source: episodes/ravi-mehta*

### 2. Three Levels of Product Work - Shreyas Doshi

- **Impact:** Customer and brand experience
- **Execution:** Hitting milestones, getting things done
- **Optics:** Creating awareness of work

Most PM-CEO conflicts stem from defaulting to different levels. Early-stage startups: optimize for execution. Platform teams: sometimes optics matters most.

*Source: episodes/shreyas-doshi*

### 3. LNO Framework - Shreyas Doshi

Categorize tasks as Leverage (10X-100X), Neutral (1X), or Overhead (<1X). Do L tasks when you're most energetic. Classic L tasks: strategy, critical PRDs, key stakeholder alignments.

*Source: episodes/shreyas-doshi*

### 4. Frontier of Understanding Framework - Ravi Mehta

Why teams fail with OKRs -- they commit to outcome goals without understanding how to move them. Four goal types based on understanding:
- **Understanding Risk:** Don't know the levers -- goal is to learn
- **Dependency Risk:** Know levers but lack resources
- **Execution Risk:** Have resources, test hypotheses
- **Strategic Risk:** Have hypothesis, it might be wrong

*Source: episodes/ravi-mehta*

### 5. Strategy Stack - Ravi Mehta

Sequential process: Mission -> Vision -> Strategy -> Roadmap -> Goals. Strategy comes BEFORE goals (not after). Includes: Vision (inspiring future), Strategic Framework (market, success, big bets), Roadmap (working backward from vision).

*Source: episodes/ravi-mehta*

## Common Mistakes

1. **Shutting people down instead of collaborating.** Jackie Bavaro saw her role as "saying no to everybody" in her first year at Asana and nearly got fired. A coach challenged her to "say yes to everything" for two weeks. (Jackie Bavaro)

2. **Blind choice towards management.** PMs assume they need to climb the management ladder, not realizing senior IC roles pay as well as doctors or lawyers. Understand multiple career paths. (Jackie Bavaro)

3. **Setting outcome goals without understanding how to move them.** Companies obsess over "outcomes over outputs" and set goals to increase retention without understanding WHY. Set "understanding goals" first. (Ravi Mehta)

4. **Letting execution mask strategy/culture problems.** Teams solve the same "execution problem" with bandaid meetings. The real problem: unclear strategy or culture that punishes helping peers. (Shreyas Doshi)

## Company Examples

**Tinder vs. Hinge** (Ravi Mehta): Same category, fundamentally different strategies. Tinder: "make single life more fun" (ongoing use). Hinge: "designed to be deleted" (temporary). This cascades into different monetization, product mechanics, and features.

**Asana** (Jackie Bavaro): 50% of engineers worked on framework rewrite. Slowed feature shipping but was necessary. Jackie stayed because she was "getting good growth opportunities even if the company didn't succeed."

**Tripadvisor** (Ravi Mehta): Users' journeys were interleaved with Google. Strategy: make trip planning happen on Tripadvisor. Problem: teams optimized locally for bookings, undermining the strategy.

**Stripe** (Shreyas Doshi): "High energy, sound judgment, low ego, and small teams." This culture enabled fast learning. Contrast: companies with high optics requirements breed endless status meetings.
