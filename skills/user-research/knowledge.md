# User Research & Customer Discovery - Knowledge Base

> Curated from 20+ episodes of Lenny's Podcast

## Frameworks

### 1. Jobs to Be Done (JTBD) - Bob Moesta (co-creator with Clay Christensen)

The Forces Framework explains customer behavior through four forces:
- **Push** -- context forcing change
- **Pull** -- attraction to new outcome
- **Friction** -- anxieties about change
- **Habit** -- resistance to leaving current solution

"Context makes the irrational rational" -- decisions only make sense when you understand the full situation.

*Source: episodes/bob-moesta*

### 2. Opportunity Solution Tree - Teresa Torres

Start with an outcome, branch into opportunities (unmet needs/pain points), then into solutions. The key: spend time in the problem space before jumping to solutions. Deconstruct large evergreen problems into smaller solvable opportunities.

*Source: episodes/teresa-torres*

### 3. Continuous Discovery System - Teresa Torres

Build regular customer feedback loops -- as little as one interview per week. Automate recruitment through in-product prompts, sales team triggers, and scheduling software. Sustainable beats project-based research.

*Source: episodes/teresa-torres*

### 4. Six-Phase Buying Journey - Bob Moesta

First thought -> Passive looking -> Active looking -> Deciding -> First use -> Ongoing use. Understanding where customers are in their journey lets you tailor engagement instead of forcing your sales process onto their natural timeline.

*Source: episodes/bob-moesta*

### 5. DATE Framework - Krithika Shankarraman

**D**iagnose (identify specific problem via funnel analysis), **A**nalyze (evaluate competitors), **T**ake a different path (choose differentiation), **E**xperiment (test, validate, scale what works).

*Source: episodes/krithika-shankarraman*

## Common Mistakes

1. **Hypothesizing jobs in a room instead of interviewing.** "One of the most dangerous things you can do is sit in a room and hypothesize what the jobs are because I guarantee you're 100% wrong." (Bob Moesta)

2. **Shallow interviewing.** Teams ask direct questions out of context ("What did you like?") instead of collecting stories ("Tell me about the last time you..."). Writing 50-question protocols kills natural conversation. (Teresa Torres)

3. **Copying playbooks without context.** Following what Stripe or OpenAI did without understanding the specific variables that made those strategies work leads to local maxima. (Krithika Shankarraman)

4. **Over-focus on vanity metrics.** Focusing on clicks, views, and MQLs instead of signups, pipeline, and revenue. Diagnose whether you have a leaky funnel before hiring demand gen. (Krithika Shankarraman)

## Company Examples

**Southern New Hampshire University** (Bob Moesta): Discovered 50-60 non-traditional students watching classes online. This revealed a market of 200,000+ students. Built a 200,000+ student online university by studying the struggling moment first.

**Intercom** (Bob Moesta): Used JTBD to discover four completely different jobs. Rather than four products, they took one product and turned off irrelevant features for different customer pathways.

**Basecamp** (Bob Moesta): Users wanted simplicity. Following best users' requests for Gantt charts would have destroyed the core value. "If you follow your best users, they'll take you up to a world that destroys the lower end."

**Retool** (Krithika Shankarraman): Diagnosed that paid social wasn't working. Pivoted to customer storytelling because they had terrific enterprise customers no competitor could replicate.
