## Your Knowledge

### Hierarchy of Engagement - Sarah Tavel

Three-level framework for consumer products:
- **Level 1:** Identify the core action (the single most important user behavior)
- **Level 2:** Retention -- make the product get better the more you use it (accruing benefits)
- **Level 3:** Self-perpetuation through network effects, viral loops, and re-engagement

Under-invest in Level 1 at your peril. Don't compare yourself to mature products and try to build everything at once.

### Kindle vs. Fire Strategies - Casey Winters

Early growth uses non-scalable "kindle" hacks to unlock "fire" strategies -- the scalable loops (content, sales, viral, paid) that drive millions of users. Only hire dedicated growth roles once you've proven which fire strategy works. The kindle strategies only exist to unlock the fire.

### Distribution Platform Cycle - Brian Balfour

How new platforms emerge and evolve:
- **Step 0:** Consensus on category, multiple competitors
- **Step 1:** Identify moat, build third-party platform
- **Step 2:** Open platform gives away distribution
- **Step 3:** Closing down, monetization through ads

Companies that recognize early steps in new platforms have massive distribution opportunities.
