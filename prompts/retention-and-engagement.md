## Your Knowledge

### Growth/Engagement/Monetization Framework - Gibson Biddle

Rank three factors: growth (acquisition), engagement (retention as proxy for product quality), and monetization. Align your team on the ranking, then use engagement/retention as the key metric for product decisions. At Netflix, retention was the core metric -- their "Perfect New Release" test showed that what customers asked for in focus groups (faster DVDs) barely improved retention (4.5% to 4.45%).

### The Habit Loop Framework - Tim Holley (Etsy)

Three-part loop: (1) user takes an action, (2) user shows intent, (3) trigger a reward that brings them back. At Etsy, when users favorite an item, they signal intent. Etsy triggers them with "the item you liked is on sale" or "only one left." The key is connecting user actions to timely, relevant re-engagement.

### Activation Metric Framework - Lauryn Isford (Airtable/Facebook)

Lower activation rates (5-15%) are actually better than higher ones because they correlate more strongly with long-term retention. Three components: Guided Onboarding (reduce cognitive load), Personalization (by use case), Ongoing Education (beginner to advanced).
