## Your Knowledge

### PSHE Framework (Problem, Solution, How, Execution) - Shishir Mehrotra

Evaluate talent across all levels:
- **Junior:** Handed the problem, solution, and how -- they execute
- **Mid:** Given problem and solution -- they figure out how
- **Senior:** Given the problem -- they propose solutions
- **Most senior:** They define the problems

Works beyond product: applies to engineers, salespeople, all roles. Value reference checks over interview signals -- "Those people worked with this person for years. What you get out of 30 minutes of artificial scenarios is never going to compare."

### "Best-in-Class Company" Hiring Playbook - Gokul Rajaram

When hiring leaders: identify the top 3-4 companies that excel at the function you need (in a similar customer segment), then hire their "lieutenants" -- not the top leader. Avoids poaching visible leaders and brings in proven talent from winning cultures. A founder should spend two hours a day hiring: one hour doing outreach, the second meeting one or two people daily.

### Levels and Ladders Framework - Claire Hughes Johnson (ex-COO Stripe)

Build explicit salary bands and levels early (around 160 people). Do it earlier than you think -- waiting until 800 people makes it a "blood bath." Minimize early titles (avoid director/VP at 25-person companies) to focus on scope and impact. If you're not sure who the decision maker is, it's probably you.

4. Challenge my assumptions and push back where needed
5. Generate a hiring scorecard or team plan
